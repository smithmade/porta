Prometheus is the Titan who in Greek Mythology, flew to the sun in order to give Man the one thing that he was forbidden; fire. When the attributes of the animals were dished out, Man was left naked and forgotten, without feathers or claws, without scales or fur. Prometheus, out of his love for Man – the bare pale animal, delivered fire so that Man may endure. Fire may be the origin of civilization and its progress. For his theft, Prometheus was punished by Zeus and condemned to eternal torment. Prometheus was bound to a rock where each day an eagle would tear out his liver with talons and beak. His liver would grow back each day to be torn out the next. Prometheus was understood to have a profound intelligence and power of forethought; he is punished for the conscious decision that he made to act against the gods, a decision that produced an infinite future for humanity. Containing fire has given us power. At times we feel the depth of what fire is capable of giving, we feel an ancient connection, and a force as if there were nothing else, as it may have been at one time – just man and fire.
